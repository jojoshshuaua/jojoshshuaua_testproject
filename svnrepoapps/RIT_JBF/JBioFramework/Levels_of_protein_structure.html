Levels of Protein Architecture

    10
      The primary structure of a protein is the linear sequence of amino acids connected in a series of peptide bonds. This picture of peptide bonds was obtained from the WWW at http://bioweb.uwlax.edu/GenWeb/Molecular/Bioinformatics/Unit_4/Lab_4-1/lab_4-1.htm.

      The primary structure is sometimes symbolized by a using the 3 - or 1 - letter abbreviations for the amino acids. For example, a short segment of the enzyme chymotrypsin has the following primary structure, represented first by full amino acid names, then by 3 -letter abbreviations and finally by 1-letter abbreviations

      Valine - Threonine - Alanine - Alanine - Histidine - Cysteine - Glycine

      VAL - THR - ALA - ALA - HIS - CYS – GLY

      V - T - A - A - H - C - G

    20
      Proteins are much more than linear polymers. The side chains on the individual amino acids enable them to form salt bridges (electrostatic interactions), hydrogen bonds, and hydrophobic interactions. Secondary structure refers to hydrogen bonding patterns between amino acids that are close to each other in the primary sequence. The alpha helix is a coiled structure in which hydrogen bonds form between the amide nitrogen of one amino acid and the carbonyl oxygen of the amino acid four residues away. Lysozyme (PDB file pdb1l48.ent) contains several alpha helices. You can see them using Chime.

      The beta sheet is a more extended secondary structure with hydrogen bonds between amide nitrogens and carbonyl oxygens on polypeptide chains that run either parallel or anti-parallel. Chymotrypsin (PDB file pdb8gch.ent) contains a lot of beta sheet.

    30
      Tertiary structure refers to longer range interactions in the polypeptide structure, including hydrogen bonds and disulfide bridges between cysteine side chains that are adjacent in the three dimensional structure of the protein.
    40
      Many proteins consist of multiple subunits (separate polypeptide chains) that are held together by non-covalent forces. This collection of non-covalent forces between subunits is called quaternary structure. For example, horse liver alcohol dehydrogenase consists of two identical subunits that are held together by non-covalent forces.
